Mathematical Talks

Join us as we take a peek at topics that most students are never told of. Learn about the patterns that even our brightest minds fail to rationalize. And discover the many secrets and mysteries in maths that we are still unaware of.
These talks are introductory-level. Anyone can attend and understand the talk, and everyone is encouraged to give them a try. Check out the timeline on this page for more details.*

No, there won't be any motivational speeches about how, if you try hard enough, you can always find your ex (x). Instead these will be sessions where we explore the beauties of mathematics, like a science show.

  • How Instruments Are Tuned Using Maths

    ...and why all modern music is just slighty out of tune. Did you know Pythagoras did more than just his famous triangle theorem? A collaboration with LyTunes.

  • And What More is There to Mathematics than Sums?

    We discuss how, in reality, mathematics is all about the human drive of curiosity. And how the web of maths is a potent tool so seemingly perfect that it seems almost divinely inspired.

  • Reimann-Zeta : The Million Dollar Problem

    And arguably one of the hardest ways to earn a million. We discuss how a proving a century old theorem (that everyone just assumes is true) has evaded the brightest and smartest mathematicians of our time.

  • P v NP. And Why All of Modern Computing Hopes It's True

    What does solving Sudoku, scheduling a Timetable, and packing a bin have in common? We discuss how solving a problem in computer science and mathematics could break the internet. Literally.

*Note: Dates subject to change.
